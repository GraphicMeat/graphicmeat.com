What is Graphic Meat? Not vegan, not a butcher — it’s software.

Every so often someone arrives here looking for sausages, or leaves worried about animals. Time to settle it: no food is produced on these premises. The meat is a metaphor, and it always has been.

Graphic Meat20 July 20264 min read

Let’s clear the counter. Graphic Meat is an independent software studio, established in 2013. We do not sell meat. We are also not a vegan alternative to meat. There is no product here you can eat, grill, marinate or return because it arrived thawed. The only thing we cure is bugs.

So why “meat”?

Because software has a texture problem. A great deal of what the industry produces is garnish: decks, mockups, roadmaps, announcements of announcements. It looks like a meal from a distance, but there is nothing to chew on.

Meat, in this kitchen, stands for the opposite — the substance of the work. The part that remains when you take away the pitch: code that compiles, an app that launches, a file that exports, a feature someone actually uses on a Tuesday. When engineers say “where’s the meat?”, they are not asking about lunch. Neither are we. The name is graphical satire: we drew the metaphor in red and hung it over the door.

Vegetarians welcome. The only thing butchered here is scope.

And why “graphic”?

Because the substance has to be made visible. Software is invisible until someone gives it a surface — an interface, a layout, a page you can hold with your eyes. “Graphic” is the promise that the meat gets plated: designed, arranged, put on screen where you can judge it. Graphic meat is the result of coding, presented.

“Well done isn’t a requirement”

Our tagline confuses waiters and delights compilers. In a restaurant, “well done” is how you ruin a steak. In software, “well done” is how you never ship one — polishing a thing forever instead of serving it. We aim for the same doneness a good kitchen does: cooked enough to be safe, rare enough to still be interesting. Ship it medium-rare, season with updates.

The honest FAQ

Is Graphic Meat vegan? There is nothing to be vegan about. No animal — and, to be fair, no vegetable — is involved in production. Our apps contain 0 g of protein and a surprising amount of fibre, in the form of source code.

Do you sell real meat? No. If you arrived here hungry, we can only offer PhotoBooks, which is free and pairs well with red wine and a folder of holiday photos.

Is the name serious? The name is a joke. The work is not. That ratio — satire on the label, substance in the package — is the whole brand.

What’s actually on the menu

Independent apps, built and served by one small studio: private by default, running on your machine, doing one thing properly. The product counter is here. Everything on it is graphic, none of it is meat, and all of it is meant to be chewed on.
